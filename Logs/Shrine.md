This shrine is dedicated to the god of art, cartography, glyphs, images, knowledge, literature, and scholars: Deneir. The shrine has four pillars that stand on a circular stone floor. The pillars are old and weathered and share a noticeable carving of a horizontal line on all of them. There is also a wooden table in the shrine. It has some small trinkets and burnt out candles and was added later by the looks of it. 

As the party steps onto the shrine the line open to reveal eyes looking at each of the party members. The pupil consists of a triangle instead of a circle and when they open a flame flares up just above the eye. 

Nothing else happens on the surface but deep below the ground. Under a massive layer of near indestructible stone, ancient Halruaan devices churn up. Linking to the players' minds.

"The river writes slowly. When it has written enough, the lost will be found."

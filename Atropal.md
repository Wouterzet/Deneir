An atropal is a ghastly, unfinished creation of an evil god, cast adrift and abandoned long ago. Since an atropal was never truly alive, it can't be raised from the dead or resurrected by any means. Even divine intervention can't breathe true life into this hateful, miserable horror.

An atropal is malformed and unfinished. Its wet, wrinkled, and bloated body is surmounted by a hairless, overlarge head set with glassy, vacant eyes. It constantly drools stinking ichor as it mouths obscenities. Its arms are too slender, with tiny hands ending in cruelly shaped nails. An atropal never walks but always floats, with its atrophied, dead legs hanging useless below it.

Negative Energy Connection. The shriveled umbilical cord of an atropal appears to trail off into nothingness, but in fact, it attaches to the Negative Plane. This connection gives the atropal power over death and undeath. The cord can be severed by a vorpal sword or other vorpal weapon, and doing so weakens the atropal's tie to the Negative Plane.

An atropal can summon vestiges of creatures that died in the Negative Plane, which manifest as wraiths. These servants can exist outside the Negative Plane only by the atropal's sheer force of will, and they quickly dissipate when the atropal is destroyed.

Undead Nature. An atropal doesn't require air, food, drink, or sleep.


# The Atropal of [[Nimbral]]

_Huge undead (titan), neutral evil_

**Armor Class** 17 (rotting divine flesh, semi-corporeal shielding)  
**Hit Points** 385 (30d12 + 180)  
**Speed** 0 ft., fly 60 ft. (hover)

STR 19 (+4) | DEX 5 (-3) | CON 22 (+6) | INT 25 (+7) | WIS 19 (+4) | CHA 24 (+7)

**Saving Throws** Con +13, Wis +11, Cha +14  
**Skills** Arcana +14, Perception +5 (disadvantage, see “Dim Sight”)  
**Senses** Life Sight 300ft., passive Perception 15  
**Languages** Understands Celestial but utters only obscene nonsense  
**Challenge** 21 (33,000 XP)

---

### Traits

**Magic Resistance.** The atropal has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.

**Negative Energy Aura.** Creatures within 300 feet of the atropal can’t regain hit points, and any creature that starts its turn there takes 21 (6d6) necrotic damage. If the atropal’s umbilical cord is severed (see Weaknesses), this aura disappears.

**Turn Immunity.** The atropal is immune to effects that turn undead.

**Immutable Form.** The atropal is immune to any spell or effect that would alter its form.

**Life Sight (Weakness).** The atropal cannot see normally, It perceives life only as pulsing sources of radiant energy. It automatically detects creatures with hit points, but it has disadvantage on attacks against creatures who suppress or disguise their life-force (e.g. hiding behind consecrated wards, invisibility combined with necrotic shrouds, or special relics the PCs can discover).

**Planar Anchor (Weakness).** The atropal is tethered to the unstable portal. Any creature who succeeds on a DC 20 Arcana check while within 30 ft. of the portal can weaken its regeneration (see below) for 1 round.

---

### Actions

**Touch.** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +11 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. _Hit:_ 21 (6d6) necrotic damage.

**Ray of Cold.** _Ranged Spell Attack:_ +14 to hit, range 120 ft., one target. _Hit:_ 27 (8d6) cold damage.

**Life Drain (Recharge 5-6).** One creature within 120 ft. must succeed on a DC 20 Con save or take 54 (12d8) necrotic damage, half on success. The atropal regains hit points equal to half damage dealt.

**Summon Wraith (Recharge 5-6).** The atropal summons 1d2 wraiths. They vanish if the atropal dies.

---

### Legendary Actions (3 per round)

- **Touch.** The atropal makes one Touch attack.
    
- **Ray of Cold (Costs 2).** Uses Ray of Cold.
    
- **Wail (Costs 3).** All creatures within 120 ft. who can hear must succeed on a DC 20 Con save or gain 1 level of exhaustion.
    

---

### Lair Actions (Portal Cataclysm)

When fighting near the unstable Nimbral portal, the atropal can take lair actions at initiative count 20 (losing ties):

- **Planar Surge.** Gravity inverts until initiative 20 next round. Creatures must make a DC 18 Strength save or be pulled 20 ft. upward.
    
- **Dimensional Echo.** A random spell cast this round repeats at initiative 20, with new targets chosen randomly.
    
- **Necrotic Storm.** All living creatures in the area take 14 (4d6) necrotic damage.
    

---

### Legendary Resistances (3/Day).

If the atropal fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.

---

## Hidden Weaknesses (for players to discover in loops)

- **The Umbilical Cord.** As in the original, if severed (via a vorpal blade or enchanted cutting tool), the aura weakens massively. In your version, the PCs might learn about this in a ruined Halruaan journal.
    
- **Dim Sight.** It only perceives life-force. PCs can find a Halruaan relic that cloaks them in necrotic energy, granting advantage on Stealth checks against it.
    
- **Planar Anchor.** Its healing/regeneration (10 HP/round baseline) can be suppressed by interacting with the unstable portal — but only if the PCs know the correct sigils to inscribe (learned from ruins).
    
- **Exhaustion Cap.** Its Wail is lethal on paper — but Halruaan notes might reveal that plugging your ears with silver dust stops the resonance (they’ll fail until they learn this).
    

---

## Why It’s “Unwinnable First Try”

- Its **Negative Energy Aura** and **Wail** alone should wipe the party without prep.
    
- Summoned wraiths + regeneration force attrition.
    
- They can’t hide normally because of its blindsight — until they learn the _life-force cloaking trick_.
    
- They need the portal notes to even understand how to weaken its regen.
    

After multiple loops, though, they’ll know:

- Sever the cord.
    
- Mask their life-force.
    
- Shut down the portal anchor.
    
- Use terrain and villager help effectively.
